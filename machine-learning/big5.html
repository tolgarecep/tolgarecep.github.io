As man's expedition with a ruler in his hand continues, he of course tried to measure the psychology. Optimizing psychological assessment is the purpose of psychometrics. Big five is an example to trait theories which is a branch of personality theories, and is among the most popular and industrially influential one. Using a technique called factor analysis, data clustered the focus around these five traits since they're seemingly most informative about one's personality: extraversion, neuroticism, agreeableness, conscientiousness and openness to experience. There is no specific way to measure big-five, ... Check out Modern Psychometrics if you're interested.

OCEAN model is generally a latter component in the process. With modeing questionarrie data or digital footprints, people are profiled in this framework and then engagement step begins, (e.g. ad manipulation). We will explore what kind of features of people that big five traits can be informative of.

In this writing, I edited and talked through various findings regarding the case, and soruce for some of the arguments is this repo at my GitHub page, where I process, analyse and model on a dataset.

Insights between the traits

In the dataset, all subjects with available big five data is from the United States. The most informative relationships in the correlation matrix are three pairs, all of which are negative correlations around -.3

  1. Neuroticism & Agreeableness
  2. Neuroticism & Extraversion
  3. Neuroticism & Conscientiousness

You can find the correlation matrix and more in the notebook.

Political orientation

The research using the data is about face images exposing political orientation (which I talked about here), but analysis regarding big-five personality traits are also available in it to compare. Big-five personalities combined can predict political orientation correctly 66% of the time (which is also what I got in the notebook), and face recognition algorithm reaches to 73% accuracy.

Political distribution is skewed towards liberals in the dataset. Most discriminating personal trait turns out to be openness; subjects who scored higher at opennes tend to be liberal. Distributions for classes of political orientation are very close to each other for other personality traits.

"Since Openness to Experience is associated with positive responses to novel stimuli . . . researchers posit that individuals high in this trait are more likely to respond favorably to liberal social policies, which often involve acceptance of unconventional behaviors, and liberal economic policies, which may involve a willingness to support proposals that entail new government involvement in the economy." (Gerber et al., 2011, p. 269)

The quick model I trained in the notebook reached to an AUC score of 66%, just as it is in the article (although I'm not sure if we had same preprocessing strategies, I kept it very simple.)

Gender

Gender is distribution is also skewed, subjects in the dataset are mostly males. It's harder to look at a distribution of a trait and guess the gender class it belongs, but we can observe that more neurotic people tend to be males.

Logistic regression scored 63% at average -average of when guessing gender from personality traits.

Age

Subjects are mostly young such that values bigger than 50 are considered outliers.

Young tend to be liberal.

Ridge regression with a little tuning of alpha parameter scored 9 RMSE.
