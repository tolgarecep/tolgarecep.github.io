wag the dog
writer's cut

"Why does a dog wag its tail? Because a dog is smarter than its tail. If the tail were smarter, the tail would wag the dog."

This captures the nature of mass manipulation, disinformation and new era of marketing perfectly. Privacy concerns of society were present way before the potential of computational psychology became visible, so I see two types of privacy breaches; hard-coded ones, cases in which your data slips to the hands of people you don't want to, and soft-coded ones; type of information gathering that is not sustained directly by your input but from new data learned from the data you gave (create, footprint you left.) Hard-coded ones is just the tip of the iceberg. Second type reminds me of some situations Sherlock Holmes frequently found himself in; he deduces some information that the person thinks is private, from observation of purely what person manifests in public. Regulating this "learning" would be a hard new venture, and an obstacle before innovation.

And even if you're given a chance to protect your data from being fed into machine learning models, there will still be another third person that needs your data and is able to take it, 'privacy settings' is nothing; nothing stops this train.
